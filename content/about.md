## Hey, I'm Jimmy.

I'm a technical artist based in Taiwan, and I'm heading to **SCAD** (Savannah College of Art and Design) to study visual effects.

My background is a bit unusual. By day I've been working as an **iOS engineer** — writing Swift, building apps, the whole software development thing. But the part of the job I always kept coming back to was the intersection of code and visuals. How do you write a system that generates something beautiful? How do you make something that's both technically correct and actually looks good on screen?

That's what led me to Houdini, and eventually to this site.

---

## What I do

Most of my work lives in **Houdini**. Particle simulations, VFX, motion design, procedural systems — that's where I spend most of my time. I've done commercial work at Moonshine Animation, which means real deadlines, real feedback, and real pressure to deliver.

I also know my way around **Nuke** for compositing, and my engineering background means I'm comfortable writing VEX or diving into a pipeline problem without panicking.

Here's the rough skill set:

- **Houdini** — FX, motion design, procedural modelling, VEX
- **Nuke** — compositing, integration
- **iOS / Swift** — yes, I actually ship apps
- **Technical pipelines** — tools, automation, that kind of thing

---

## Why this site

Honestly, I started this because I kept forgetting things. You figure out how something works in Houdini, you move on to the next project, and six months later you're staring at the same problem again with no memory of how you solved it.

So this is partly a personal reference — a place to write things down properly so I don't have to re-figure them out. If it's useful to someone else too, even better.

The name *Learning Lab* is pretty literal. I'm still figuring things out. That's kind of the point.
